About The App

One place for live NHL projections, player context, RAPM, and matchup tools.

This app combines NHL schedule data, lineup scraping, static RAPM/context exports, live game models, and player projection tables into one workflow. The glossary tab below collects the short forms used across skaters, goalies, projections, standings, and lineup tools so the metrics are easier to read quickly.

What The App Covers

The app brings together game projections, lineup-driven playoff odds, player cards, shooting and Edge views, RAPM, context metrics, and team-level comparisons. Most pages use the same shared team and season state so it is easy to move from a player page into team or game context without resetting the workflow.

Projections Current game and playoff probabilities, lineup-based team totals, and centered player projection values.
Skaters And Goalies Player cards, RAPM, context, shooting breakdowns, Edge data, and season tables.
Team And Line Tools Roster context, line combinations, pairings, and usage-driven comparisons.

How To Read The Metrics

Most metrics are shown either as raw values, rates, percentiles, or centered projection values. Context metrics such as QoT, QoC, and ZS describe deployment, while RAPM attempts to isolate player impact by adjusting for teammates and opponents. Projection values on the current skater pages are centered, so positive values indicate players projected above baseline and negative values indicate players projected below baseline.
