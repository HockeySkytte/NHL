About The App

Built for nights when you want the schedule, the matchup context, and the player detail in the same place.

The app pulls together live NHL schedule data, lineup updates, team context, player cards, RAPM, and game projections without forcing you to hop between separate tools. The goal is simple: get from a slate view to a player decision quickly, with enough context to trust the read.

Game projections Player context RAPM and usage Line and team views

How most people use it

Start with the slate or standings, move into skaters or goalies for player-level context, then use the line and team pages to check deployment, matchup environment, and shot quality. The glossary stays here as the quick reference when a page gets dense.

What You Can Do Here

Most pages share the same team and season controls, so the workflow stays consistent while you move between schedule, player, and team views. That makes it easier to compare a projection with the underlying player profile instead of treating every page like a separate product.

Work the slate Use projections for win probability, lineup-aware expectations, and the broader game environment before drilling into the player pages.
Check the player case Skaters and goalies combine cards, shooting or goaltending views, RAPM, and context metrics so you can see production, usage, and quality together.
Validate the environment Team and line views help confirm how a player is being deployed, who he is running with, and where the chance generation is actually coming from.

A lot of the value is in moving between those views quickly. The app is meant to be a working desk, not a single headline number.

A Few Reading Notes

Raw counts tell you volume. Rates help normalize usage. Context metrics such as QoT, QoC, and ZS describe the deployment behind the results, while RAPM is there to separate player impact from teammate and opponent effects. On the projection side, centered values are relative to baseline: positive means above expectation, negative means below it.

If a page feels abbreviation-heavy, use the glossary tab as the decoder ring. It collects the terms that repeat across skaters, goalies, projections, exports, and tables.
